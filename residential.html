Residential Project

A transformative 5-day residential experience focused on environmental conservation and community building, bringing together young people from diverse backgrounds.

Killarney National Park, Co. Kerry

Duration

5 days, 4 nights

Group Size

24 participants from across Ireland

Focus Theme

Environmental Conservation & Sustainability

Project Experience

Our residential project brought together 24 young adults for an intensive week of environmental conservation work, team building, and personal development in one of Ireland's most beautiful national parks.

Key Activities & Achievements

Environmental Conservation

Trail Restoration Project

Repaired and maintained 2km of walking trails, including erosion control and path stabilization to protect the natural landscape.

Invasive Species Management

Removed over 500 invasive rhododendron plants to protect native Irish flora and restore natural habitats.

Wildlife Monitoring

Participated in red deer population surveys and bird watching programs to support ongoing conservation research.

Personal Development

Leadership Skills

Led a team of 6 participants in the trail restoration project, developing delegation and project management skills.

Cultural Awareness

Worked alongside participants from diverse backgrounds, enhancing cultural understanding and communication skills.

Problem Solving

Developed creative solutions to environmental challenges and group dynamics, improving adaptability and critical thinking.

Impact & Reflection

"The residential project was a transformative experience that showed me the power of collective action and the importance of environmental stewardship. Working alongside passionate young people from across Ireland gave me a deeper understanding of our shared responsibility to protect our natural heritage."
